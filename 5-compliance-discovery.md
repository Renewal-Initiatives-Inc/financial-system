**GAAP Policy Documentation (Required for Future Audited Financials):**
- **Capitalization Policy:** Document threshold for capitalizing vs. expensing fixed asset purchases (e.g., $1,000 minimum? $5,000?). GAAP requires consistent application. Policy affects when costs flow through Construction in Progress (D-032) vs. expense accounts (D-031). Must document useful lives and depreciation methods by asset class (building, equipment, vehicles, furniture). Extends D-019/D-020 depreciation structure with formal policy documentation.
- **Bad Debt/Allowance Policy:** Document methodology for estimating uncollectible AR (tenant rent receivables from D-026). Options: direct write-off method (simple, used by small nonprofits) vs. allowance method (GAAP-preferred for audits, requires estimating uncollectible percentage based on aging). If allowance method chosen, create "Allowance for Doubtful Accounts" contra-asset account. Policy must specify write-off authorization process and timing.
- **Functional Allocation Methodology:** Document how Program/Admin/Fundraising expense split is calculated for 990 reporting (extends D-018 year-end allocation). Must specify: allocation basis (time tracking, headcount, square footage, direct assignment), allocation frequency (monthly, quarterly, year-end), approval process. GAAP requires "reasonable and consistent" allocation methodology. IRS expects functional expenses on 990 Line 24 to reconcile to audited financials.
- **Depreciation Policy:** Formalize depreciation useful lives and methods (building 27.5-39 years straight-line per IRS, equipment/vehicles 5-7 years, furniture 7 years). Document component depreciation approach for building (roof, HVAC, structure have different lives). Policy supports D-019/D-020 AI depreciation assistant implementation. Required for GAAP audits and tax credit compliance (Historic Tax Credit, Historic Tax Credit substantiation).

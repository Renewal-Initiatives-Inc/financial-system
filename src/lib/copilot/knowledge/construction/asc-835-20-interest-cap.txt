Source: ASC 835-20 — Interest: Capitalization of Interest; SFAS 34

ASC 835-20 — Interest Capitalization During Construction

OVERVIEW
Interest costs incurred during the acquisition, construction, or production of qualifying assets should be capitalized as part of the cost of the asset, rather than expensed.

QUALIFYING ASSETS (835-20-15-5)
- Assets being constructed for the entity's own use
- Assets intended for sale or lease that are constructed as discrete projects
- NOT: inventories routinely produced, assets ready for use, land not being developed

CAPITALIZATION PERIOD
Begins when ALL three conditions are met:
1. Expenditures for the asset have been made
2. Activities necessary to prepare the asset for intended use are in progress
3. Interest cost is being incurred

Ends when the asset is substantially complete and ready for intended use (PIS date).

AMOUNT TO CAPITALIZE
- Avoidable interest: interest that theoretically could have been avoided if construction expenditures had not been made
- Cannot exceed actual interest incurred during the period
- Computed using weighted-average accumulated expenditures × appropriate interest rate

INTEREST RATE
- Specific borrowing rate: if funds borrowed specifically for construction, use that rate first
- Weighted-average rate: for additional expenditures beyond specific borrowings, use weighted-average rate on other outstanding borrowings

DISCLOSURE
Must disclose:
- Total interest cost incurred during the period
- Amount capitalized
- Amount expensed

RI-SPECIFIC CONTEXT
RI's construction project involves the AHP loan ($3.5M at below-market rate). During construction:
- Interest on AHP loan is capitalized to CIP (Construction in Progress)
- Interest is allocated across CIP cost codes proportionally
- At PIS date, capitalized interest becomes part of the building cost basis
- Post-PIS date, AHP loan interest is expensed (program service expense)

The system tracks capitalized vs. expensed interest through the GL engine. CIP cost codes categorize interest as SOFT_COST.

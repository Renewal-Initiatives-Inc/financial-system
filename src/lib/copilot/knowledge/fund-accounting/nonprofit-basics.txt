Nonprofit Fund Accounting Basics

FUND ACCOUNTING vs COMMERCIAL ACCOUNTING
Fund accounting tracks resources by purpose/restriction, not just by entity. Each fund is a self-balancing set of accounts (assets = liabilities + net assets). This allows:
- Tracking compliance with donor restrictions
- Reporting on multiple grants/programs separately
- Demonstrating proper stewardship of restricted resources

FUND TYPES
1. General Fund (Operating Fund) — unrestricted resources for day-to-day operations
2. Restricted Funds — resources subject to donor-imposed restrictions
3. Board-Designated Funds — unrestricted resources set aside by the board (internally designated, NOT donor restricted)
4. Capital/Plant Fund — tracks fixed assets and related debt
5. Endowment Fund — permanently restricted principal

CHART OF ACCOUNTS CONSIDERATIONS
- Account codes should allow reporting by natural class AND by function
- Common structure: [Type]-[Fund]-[Natural Class] or flat with sub-accounts
- RI uses a flat structure with fund ID on each transaction line

DOUBLE-ENTRY IN FUND ACCOUNTING
Every journal entry must:
1. Balance debits and credits (standard double-entry)
2. Be assigned to a specific fund
3. If affecting multiple funds, use interfund transfers

INTERFUND TRANSACTIONS
- Interfund transfers: move resources between funds (e.g., unrestricted to restricted for matching)
- Interfund loans: temporary lending between funds (should be repaid)
- Both must be properly authorized and documented

FINANCIAL STATEMENTS FOR NONPROFITS
1. Statement of Financial Position (balance sheet)
2. Statement of Activities (income statement equivalent)
3. Statement of Functional Expenses (required for voluntary health & welfare orgs; recommended for all)
4. Statement of Cash Flows
5. Notes to Financial Statements

KEY RATIOS
- Program expense ratio: program expenses / total expenses (benchmark: 65-85%)
- Current ratio: current assets / current liabilities
- Operating reserve ratio: unrestricted net assets / annual operating expenses
- Fundraising efficiency: donations / fundraising expenses

RI-SPECIFIC CONTEXT
RI is a small nonprofit with simple fund structure: one unrestricted fund (General Fund) and several restricted funds for construction grants. The system enforces fund assignment on every transaction line to maintain proper tracking.

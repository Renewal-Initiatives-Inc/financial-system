Donor Restrictions and Fund Accounting

TYPES OF DONOR RESTRICTIONS
1. Purpose restrictions: donor specifies how the funds must be used
   - Example: "For construction of affordable housing units"
   - Released when the specified purpose is accomplished

2. Time restrictions: donor specifies when funds may be used
   - Example: "For use in fiscal year 2026"
   - Released when the time period arrives or passes
   - Includes implied time restrictions on long-lived assets (see below)

3. Perpetual restrictions: donor stipulates the principal must be maintained
   - Example: endowment funds
   - Only investment return (per spending policy) may be used
   - RI does not currently have endowment funds

CONDITIONAL vs UNCONDITIONAL CONTRIBUTIONS (ASC 958-605)
- Unconditional: recognized as revenue when received/pledged
  - Restricted: recognized in "with donor restrictions" revenue
  - Unrestricted: recognized in "without donor restrictions" revenue

- Conditional: NOT recognized until conditions are substantially met
  - Recorded as refundable advance (liability) until conditions met
  - Conditions include: measurable barrier + right of return/release
  - Example: government grant requiring specific milestones

DISTINGUISHING CONDITIONS FROM RESTRICTIONS
Both may appear in grant agreements. Key test:
- Restriction: limits USE after the gift is made (purpose, time)
- Condition: must be MET before the contribution is recognized
- A single grant may have both: condition to earn it, restriction on how to use it

IMPLIED TIME RESTRICTION ON LONG-LIVED ASSETS
When donors fund acquisition of long-lived assets without explicit time restriction:
- Accounting policy election: treat as restricted until asset is placed in service, OR over the useful life of the asset
- RI policy: restricted until placed in service (released at PIS date)

RI-SPECIFIC CONTEXT
Key restricted funds for RI:
- CPA Fund: government grant with purpose restriction (affordable housing)
- MassDev Fund: government grant with purpose restriction (development)
- HTC Equity Fund: historic tax credit equity with purpose restriction (historic preservation)
- MassSave Fund: energy rebate funds for energy efficiency improvements
Each fund tracks compliance with donor/grantor requirements. Net asset releases occur when construction milestones are met or the asset is placed in service.

Note: AHP loan proceeds are unrestricted per board resolution (2026-02). The AHP loan is tracked as a liability (account 2500) under General Fund, not as a separate restricted fund.

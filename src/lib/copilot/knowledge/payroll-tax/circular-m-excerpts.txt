Massachusetts State Income Tax Withholding

TAX RATE
Massachusetts has a flat income tax rate: 5.00% (effective TY2024+)
Note: MA passed a "millionaire's tax" (4% surtax on income over $1M) effective TY2023, but this does not affect withholding for typical nonprofit employees.

WITHHOLDING COMPUTATION
1. Determine gross wages for pay period
2. Subtract exemption amount based on pay frequency and exemptions claimed on Form M-4
3. Multiply taxable wages by 5.00%
4. Result is state income tax to withhold

FORM M-4 (Massachusetts Employee's Withholding Exemption Certificate)
- Similar to federal W-4 but simpler
- Claims personal exemptions (single: 1, married: 2, head of household: 1)
- Additional exemptions for blindness and age 65+
- Each exemption reduces taxable wages by exemption amount per pay period

EXEMPTION AMOUNTS (2025, per pay period):
Weekly: $16.90 per exemption
Biweekly: $33.80 per exemption
Semimonthly: $36.60 per exemption
Monthly: $73.30 per exemption

DEPOSIT REQUIREMENTS
- Due date: 15th of following month (for most small employers)
- If withholding > $100/month: semi-monthly deposits
- Electronic filing required for employers withholding > $50,000/year
- MassTaxConnect portal for filing and payments

FORM M-941 (Massachusetts Employer's Quarterly Return)
Due: same dates as federal Form 941
Reports: total wages, MA income tax withheld, withholding liability schedule

PFML (Paid Family and Medical Leave)
- Employer contribution rate: set annually by DFML
- Split between employer and employee per law
- Reported on Form PFML-1 (quarterly)
- Applies to all employers with 25+ covered individuals

RI-SPECIFIC CONTEXT
RI is a small MA employer (2-5 employees). Monthly withholding deposits are likely sufficient. Must file Form M-941 quarterly via MassTaxConnect. PFML contributions may apply depending on covered individual count (includes 1099 contractors in the count).

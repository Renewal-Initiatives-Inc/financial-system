Source: IRS Publication 15-T — Federal Income Tax Withholding Methods (2025)

Federal Income Tax Withholding

OVERVIEW
Employers must withhold federal income tax from employee wages based on:
1. Employee's Form W-4 (filing status, adjustments)
2. Pay frequency (weekly, biweekly, semimonthly, monthly)
3. Withholding tables or percentage method

POST-2020 W-4 METHOD (current)
Step 1: Adjust annual wage amount
- Multiply per-period wage by number of pay periods
- Add other income from W-4 Step 4(a)
- Subtract deductions from W-4 Step 4(b) (or standard deduction if blank)

Step 2: Determine tentative withholding from tables
- Use filing status (Single/MFJ/HoH)
- Apply graduated rate brackets

Step 3: Apply tax credits
- Subtract per-period credit from W-4 Step 3 (dependents)

Step 4: Final per-period withholding
- Cannot be less than zero

2025 WITHHOLDING BRACKETS (Single)
Over $0 — 10%
Over $11,925 — 12%
Over $48,475 — 22%
Over $103,350 — 24%
Over $197,300 — 32%
Over $250,525 — 35%
Over $626,350 — 37%

SUPPLEMENTAL WAGES
- Aggregate method or flat 22% rate (for supplemental wages under $1M)
- Bonuses, commissions, overtime may be treated as supplemental

DEPOSIT SCHEDULES
- Monthly depositor: deposit by 15th of following month (total tax liability < $50,000 in lookback period)
- Semi-weekly depositor: deposit by Wednesday (for Sat-Tue paydays) or Friday (for Wed-Fri paydays)
- $100,000 next-day deposit rule: if accumulate $100,000+ on any day
- Electronic deposit via EFTPS required

FORM 941 — QUARTERLY RETURN
Due: April 30, July 31, October 31, January 31
Reports: total wages, federal income tax withheld, Social Security/Medicare taxes
Small employer exception: Form 944 (annual) if notified by IRS

RI-SPECIFIC CONTEXT
RI has 2-5 employees. Likely qualifies as monthly depositor (small payroll). Files Form 941 quarterly. Key employees: Executive Director (likely salaried), bookkeeper (likely part-time/hourly).

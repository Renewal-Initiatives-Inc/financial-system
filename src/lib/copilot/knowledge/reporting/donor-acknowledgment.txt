Source: IRC § 170(f)(8); IRS Publication 1771

Donor Acknowledgment Requirements

WRITTEN ACKNOWLEDGMENT (§ 170(f)(8))
Required for any single contribution of $250 or more. The acknowledgment must contain:

1. Name of the organization
2. Amount of cash contribution, AND/OR description (but not value) of non-cash contribution
3. Statement of whether organization provided goods or services in exchange
4. If goods/services provided: description and good faith estimate of value
5. If only intangible religious benefits: statement to that effect

TIMING
"Contemporaneous" means the donor must obtain the acknowledgment by the EARLIER of:
- The date the tax return is filed, or
- The due date (including extensions) for filing the return

Best practice: issue acknowledgments by January 31 for prior-year gifts.

QUID PRO QUO DISCLOSURE (§ 6115)
If a donor makes a payment exceeding $75 that is partly a contribution and partly for goods/services:
- Organization must provide written disclosure
- Must inform donor that deductible amount is limited to excess over value of goods/services
- Must provide good faith estimate of goods/services value
- Penalty: $10 per failure, up to $5,000 per fundraising event

EXCEPTIONS (no acknowledgment required):
- Government grants and contracts (these are not "contributions" under § 170)
- Goods/services of insubstantial value (2025: $12.90 or less, or goods bearing org's name with cost <= $64.70)
- Membership benefits: annual dues of $75 or less with only token benefits

NON-CASH CONTRIBUTIONS
- Organization provides description only (NOT value) on acknowledgment
- Donor determines FMV and reports on Form 8283 (for > $500)
- Over $5,000: donor must get qualified appraisal
- Organization signs Section B of Form 8283 for non-cash over $5,000

FORM 8282 (DONEE INFORMATION RETURN)
If organization sells/disposes of donated property within 3 years:
- Must file Form 8282 within 125 days of disposition
- Applies to items valued at > $5,000 on donor's Form 8283
- Reports sale price/disposition method

RI-SPECIFIC CONTEXT
RI receives mostly government grants (not subject to donor acknowledgment rules) and individual/corporate donations. For individual/corporate gifts of $250+, the system should support generating annual donor acknowledgment letters. The donors table tracks contact information needed for these letters.

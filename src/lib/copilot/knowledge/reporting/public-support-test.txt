Source: IRC § 509(a); Treas. Reg. § 1.509(a)-3; Schedule A Part II

Public Support Test Monitoring

OVERVIEW
RI must maintain public charity status by meeting the 33-1/3% public support test on Schedule A Part II. This requires ongoing monitoring of the public support ratio.

THE TEST
Public Support (Line 6) / Total Support (Line 11) >= 33.33%

PUBLIC SUPPORT INCLUDES:
- Government grants (Line 3) — full credit, no per-donor cap
- Public contributions (Line 1) — subject to universal 2% cap

TOTAL SUPPORT INCLUDES:
- All public support
- Membership fees (Line 2)
- Investment income (Line 4)
- Net UBI (Line 5)
- Tax revenues (Line 6 of Section B)
- Program service revenue (Line 10a) — critical: includes RENTAL INCOME

THE 2% CAP (Line 5)
- Applies to ALL donors on Line 1 (not just related parties)
- Calculate: 2% × Total Support (Line 11)
- For each donor: excess over 2% threshold is excluded from public support
- Aggregation rule: related persons per § 4946(a)(1)(C)-(G) combined as one donor

UNUSUAL GRANT EXCLUSION (Reg. 1.509(a)-3(c)(4))
An unusually large grant can be excluded from both numerator and denominator if:
1. Attracted by publicly supported programs (not personal relationships)
2. Unusually large relative to normal support
3. Would adversely affect public support status
4. No material restriction on use
5. From disinterested party
Useful safety valve if one large grant would distort the ratio.

RI RISK TIMELINE:
- FY2025-2028 (construction): mostly government grants → ratio strong
- FY2028+ (operations): rental income enters denominator (Line 10a)
  - Rental income is program service revenue, NOT public support
  - Government grants may decrease (construction complete)
  - Public support percentage will decline naturally
- FY2030 (estimated): first real test after 5-year grace period
  - If below 33.33%, check facts-and-circumstances test (10% floor)
  - Consider: increase public solicitation, seek government operating grants

MONITORING RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. Track public support ratio annually (even if not yet required)
2. Maintain contribution source type tags (GOVERNMENT/PUBLIC/RELATED_PARTY)
3. Model future ratios when planning revenue mix
4. Begin diversifying funding sources before FY2030
5. Consider unusual grant exclusion if applicable

Source: IRC § 168; Treas. Reg. § 1.168(a)-1; IRS Publication 946

IRC § 168 — Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System (MACRS)

OVERVIEW
MACRS is the tax depreciation system for tangible property placed in service after 1986. Key elements:
- Recovery period (useful life class)
- Depreciation method (declining balance or straight-line)
- Convention (half-year, mid-quarter, mid-month)

RECOVERY PERIODS FOR NONPROFIT HOUSING
- 5-year property: computers, office equipment, appliances
- 7-year property: office furniture, fixtures
- 15-year property: land improvements (parking lots, landscaping, sidewalks)
- 27.5-year property: residential rental property (the building)
- Land: NOT depreciable

DEPRECIATION METHODS
- 200% declining balance (GDS): 5-year and 7-year property
- 150% declining balance (GDS): 15-year property
- Straight-line (GDS): 27.5-year residential rental property
- Straight-line (ADS): required for certain tax-exempt use property

CONVENTIONS
- Half-year convention: default for personal property (5, 7-year)
  - First year: half-year of depreciation regardless of when placed in service
  - Year of disposition: half-year of depreciation
- Mid-quarter convention: if >40% of personal property placed in service in Q4
- Mid-month convention: real property (27.5-year)
  - Depreciation starts mid-month of PIS date

TAX-EXEMPT ORGANIZATIONS
501(c)(3) organizations are generally exempt from income tax, so MACRS is primarily relevant for:
1. GAAP book depreciation (where MACRS useful lives provide reasonable estimates)
2. Unrelated business income property
3. Form 990 reporting (Part X, Line 10)

SECTION 179 AND BONUS DEPRECIATION
- § 179 immediate expensing: generally NOT available to tax-exempt organizations (must have taxable income to use)
- Bonus depreciation (§ 168(k)): phasing down from 100% (2022) to 0% (2027). Not relevant for nonprofits unless they have UBTI.

RI-SPECIFIC CONTEXT
RI's primary depreciable asset will be the residential rental building (27.5-year, straight-line, mid-month convention). Component allocation at PIS date:
- Building structure: 27.5-year
- Building systems (HVAC, plumbing, electrical): 27.5-year (as part of building)
- Land improvements: 15-year
- Personal property (appliances, fixtures): 5-7 year
- Land: not depreciable (must be separated from building cost)

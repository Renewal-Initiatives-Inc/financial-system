Source: ASC 360-10 — Property, Plant, and Equipment; ASC 360-10-35

ASC 360 — Fixed Asset Accounting and Impairment

INITIAL MEASUREMENT
Property, plant, and equipment recorded at cost, which includes:
- Purchase price or construction cost
- Directly attributable costs to bring asset to working condition
- Capitalized interest (ASC 835-20) during construction period

SUBSEQUENT MEASUREMENT
- Carry at cost less accumulated depreciation
- No revaluation to fair value under US GAAP (unlike IFRS)
- Impairment testing when indicators exist

DEPRECIATION (ASC 360-10-35-4)
- Systematic and rational allocation of cost over useful life
- Methods: straight-line, declining balance, units of production
- Nonprofits: straight-line is most common and appropriate
- Review useful life estimates periodically; change in estimate applied prospectively

IMPAIRMENT TESTING (ASC 360-10-35-17)
When to test: when events or changes in circumstances indicate carrying amount may not be recoverable.

Indicators of impairment:
- Significant decrease in market value
- Significant change in how asset is used
- Physical damage
- Adverse legal/regulatory changes
- Operating or cash flow losses associated with the asset

Two-step test:
1. Recoverability test: compare carrying amount to sum of undiscounted future cash flows
   - If carrying amount <= undiscounted cash flows → no impairment
   - If carrying amount > undiscounted cash flows → proceed to step 2
2. Measurement: impairment loss = carrying amount - fair value

For nonprofits using assets in operations (not held for sale):
- Use service capacity concept rather than cash flows for recoverability
- Impairment exists when asset can no longer provide the level of service for which it was acquired

HELD FOR SALE (ASC 360-10-45-9)
Assets classified as held for sale when:
- Management commits to a plan to sell
- Asset available for immediate sale
- Active program to locate buyer initiated
- Sale probable within one year
- Asset actively marketed at reasonable price
Measured at lower of carrying amount or fair value less cost to sell.

RI-SPECIFIC CONTEXT
RI's primary fixed asset (residential property) is used in operations (providing housing), not held for investment. Impairment testing uses service capacity approach. Given the property serves a charitable purpose with stable demand for affordable housing, impairment risk is low unless physical damage or regulatory changes occur.

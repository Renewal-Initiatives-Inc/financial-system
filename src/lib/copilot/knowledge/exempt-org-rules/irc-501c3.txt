Source: IRC § 501(c)(3); Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1

IRC § 501(c)(3) — Tax-Exempt Status for Charitable Organizations

OVERVIEW
Organizations described in IRC § 501(c)(3) are exempt from federal income tax if organized and operated exclusively for religious, charitable, scientific, educational, or other specified exempt purposes. "Exclusively" is interpreted as "primarily" per Treas. Reg. § 1.501(c)(3)-1(c)(1).

ORGANIZATIONAL TEST (§ 1.501(c)(3)-1(b))
- Articles of organization must limit purposes to exempt purposes
- Must not expressly empower activities that are not in furtherance of exempt purposes (more than an insubstantial part)
- Dissolution clause: assets must be dedicated to exempt purpose upon dissolution

OPERATIONAL TEST (§ 1.501(c)(3)-1(c))
- Must be operated exclusively (primarily) for exempt purposes
- No part of net earnings may inure to benefit of private shareholders or individuals
- May not participate or intervene in political campaigns (absolute prohibition)
- Substantial part of activities may not be carrying on propaganda or attempting to influence legislation (unless § 501(h) election made)

PRIVATE BENEFIT / PRIVATE INUREMENT
- Private inurement (§ 1.501(c)(3)-1(c)(2)): no part of net earnings inures to insiders (officers, directors, key employees). Any amount triggers loss of exemption.
- Private benefit doctrine: broader than inurement. Organization must serve public rather than private interests. Evaluated qualitatively and quantitatively — must be incidental in both.

RI-SPECIFIC CONTEXT
Renewal Initiatives, Inc. is a Massachusetts 501(c)(3) classified as a public charity under IRC § 170(b)(1)(A)(vi). The organization manages a single property development and housing operation. Key compliance areas:
- Housing operations serve charitable purpose (providing affordable housing)
- Developer fee arrangement must not constitute private inurement
- Board member transactions require arm's-length terms and conflict-of-interest review
- No political campaign activity permitted

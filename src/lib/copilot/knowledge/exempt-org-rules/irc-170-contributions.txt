Source: IRC § 170; Treas. Reg. § 1.170A-1; IRS Publication 1771

IRC § 170 — Charitable Contributions

DONOR DEDUCTION RULES
Individuals may deduct charitable contributions to qualified 501(c)(3) organizations, subject to AGI limitations:
- Cash contributions: up to 60% of AGI (§ 170(b)(1)(G))
- Capital gain property: up to 30% of AGI (§ 170(b)(1)(C))
- Excess contributions carry forward 5 years (§ 170(d)(1))

SUBSTANTIATION REQUIREMENTS (§ 170(f)(8))
For contributions of $250 or more, donor must obtain contemporaneous written acknowledgment from the donee organization. The acknowledgment must state:
1. Amount of cash and description of non-cash property contributed
2. Whether organization provided goods or services in exchange
3. Description and good faith estimate of value of goods/services provided (if any)
4. If goods/services consist solely of intangible religious benefits, a statement to that effect

"Contemporaneous" means obtained by the earlier of: (a) the date the return is filed, or (b) the due date (including extensions) for filing the return.

QUID PRO QUO CONTRIBUTIONS (§ 6115)
If a donor makes a payment exceeding $75 that is partly a contribution and partly for goods/services, the organization must provide a written disclosure statement that:
1. Informs the donor that the deductible amount is limited to the excess of the payment over the value of goods/services
2. Provides a good faith estimate of the value of goods/services

PENALTY: $10 per failure, up to $5,000 per fundraising event or mailing.

NONCASH CONTRIBUTIONS
- Over $500: donor files Form 8283 (§ 170(f)(11)(B))
- Over $5,000: qualified appraisal required (§ 170(f)(11)(C))
- Over $500,000: appraisal must be attached to return (§ 170(f)(11)(D))

DONEE REPORTING (Form 8282)
If organization sells, exchanges, or disposes of donated property within 3 years of receipt (and property was valued over $5,000), must file Form 8282 within 125 days.

RI-SPECIFIC CONTEXT
Most RI contributions are government grants (AHP, CPA, MassDev) which are not subject to the donor acknowledgment rules — those apply to individual/corporate donors. For individual and corporate donors, RI must issue written acknowledgments for gifts of $250+ and provide quid pro quo disclosures for payments over $75.

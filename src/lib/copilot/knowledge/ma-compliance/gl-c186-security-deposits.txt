Source: M.G.L. c. 186, §§ 15B, 15C; MA Attorney General's Guide

Massachusetts Tenant Security Deposit Law (G.L. c. 186, § 15B)

MAXIMUM DEPOSIT
- Landlord may require no more than first month's rent, last month's rent, security deposit equal to one month's rent, and lock change cost
- Security deposit cannot exceed one month's rent

ESCROW REQUIREMENTS
- Must be held in a separate, interest-bearing escrow account in a Massachusetts bank
- Account must be in tenant's name or clearly identified as tenant funds
- Landlord must provide tenant with receipt including: bank name, account number, amount deposited
- Receipt must be provided within 30 days of receiving deposit

INTEREST OBLIGATIONS
- Landlord must pay tenant 5% annual interest OR the actual bank interest rate, whichever is less
- Interest paid annually (on anniversary of tenancy or within 30 days after)
- If landlord fails to pay interest, tenant may deduct from rent

STATEMENT OF CONDITION (§ 15B(2))
- Landlord must provide written statement of condition within 10 days of tenancy start
- Must itemize existing damage/conditions
- Tenant has 15 days to add to or modify the statement
- Both parties should sign and retain copies

RETURN OF DEPOSIT (§ 15B(4))
- Must return deposit (plus interest) within 30 days after termination of tenancy
- Deductions allowed only for:
  1. Unpaid rent (not last month's rent if prepaid)
  2. Unpaid water charges (if tenant responsible)
  3. Damage beyond normal wear and tear
  4. Unpaid real estate tax increases (if lease provides)
- Must provide itemized list of deductions with supporting documentation

PENALTIES FOR VIOLATION
- Treble damages (3x the deposit amount) if landlord:
  - Fails to hold in escrow
  - Fails to provide receipt
  - Fails to return within 30 days
  - Makes improper deductions
  - Fails to transfer when property sold

FORM PC AND AG REPORTING
Security deposits are trust funds and should be reported as restricted liabilities. The MA AG's office may review handling during charity audits.

RI-SPECIFIC CONTEXT
RI manages residential tenants with security deposits. The system tracks:
- Security deposit amount per tenant
- Escrow bank reference
- Deposit date
- Interest rate
- Statement of condition date
- Tenancy anniversary (for annual interest payment)
All security deposit funds are held in escrow and recorded as restricted liabilities on the balance sheet.

Source: Massachusetts Attorney General's Office — Form PC Instructions

Form PC — Public Charities Annual Report

OVERVIEW
All public charities registered in Massachusetts must file Form PC annually with the Attorney General's Non-Profit Organizations/Public Charities Division. This is in ADDITION to federal Form 990 filing.

WHO MUST FILE
- Any organization soliciting or receiving contributions in Massachusetts
- Any organization holding assets for charitable purposes in Massachusetts
- Exemptions: religious organizations, educational institutions (certain), organizations with gross revenue under $5,000 (may file short form)

FILING REQUIREMENTS
Tier 1 (gross revenue < $200K and total assets < $500K):
- Form PC Short Form
- Financial statements (may be unaudited)
- Copy of IRS Form 990 or 990-EZ

Tier 2 (gross revenue $200K-$500K):
- Form PC Long Form
- Financial statements reviewed by independent CPA
- Copy of IRS Form 990

Tier 3 (gross revenue > $500K):
- Form PC Long Form
- Financial statements audited by independent CPA
- Copy of IRS Form 990

DUE DATE
Same as federal return: 4.5 months after fiscal year end
- June 30 FY → November 15
- Extensions available (file extension request with AG office)

REQUIRED INFORMATION
Part A: Organization information (name, EIN, address, officers)
Part B: Financial information
  - Revenue by source (contributions, program service, investment, other)
  - Expenses by function (program, management, fundraising)
  - Assets, liabilities, net assets
Part C: Governance
  - Board composition and meetings
  - Conflict of interest policy
  - Compensation review process
Part D: Professional fundraiser information (if applicable)

REGISTRATION
- Initial registration: Form 1 (Registration Statement)
- Annual renewal: Form PC serves as renewal
- $150 registration fee (may be waived for small organizations)
- Failure to register: may result in cease-and-desist order for solicitation

RI-SPECIFIC CONTEXT
RI is currently in Tier 1 (pre-construction, small revenue). Once the property is placed in service and assets exceed $500K, RI moves to Tier 2 or 3, requiring CPA review or audit. The system should track filing deadlines and produce the financial data needed for Form PC.

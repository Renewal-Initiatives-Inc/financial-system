**GAAP Financial Statement Presentation (Required for Future Audited Financials):**
- **Statement of Financial Position (Balance Sheet):** FASB ASC 958-210 requires nonprofit-specific presentation: Assets, Liabilities, Net Assets (split "With Donor Restrictions" / "Without Donor Restrictions" per D-014). Must show liquidity classification (current vs. noncurrent). Board currently receives balance sheet; GAAP format adds net asset split visibility.
- **Statement of Activities (Income Statement/P&L):** FASB ASC 958-225 requires showing revenue, expenses, and changes in net assets by restriction class. Must show net asset releases (D-029 automatic releases visible here). Functional expense categories (Program/Admin/Fundraising per D-018) displayed. Board currently receives P&L; GAAP format adds net asset change reconciliation.
- **Statement of Cash Flows:** FASB ASC 958-230 requires indirect method showing operating, investing, financing activities. Must reconcile change in net assets to cash from operations. New requirement for board (not currently produced in QuickBooks).
- **Statement of Functional Expenses:** FASB ASC 958-720 requires nonprofits to show expenses by both nature (salaries, rent, utilities per D-031) AND function (Program/Admin/Fundraising per D-018). Typically presented as matrix. Required for Form 990 reconciliation. New requirement for board.
- **Notes to Financial Statements:** Significant accounting policies (Chunk 5 policy documentation), restricted net assets detail (fund-level breakdown per D-013), loan disclosure (D-022 AHP loan terms), fixed asset schedule (D-019 depreciation), grant commitments, contingencies.
- **Decision needed:** Should board reports use full GAAP presentation now (future-proofs for audits), or simplified presentation with GAAP formatting added later when audit required?

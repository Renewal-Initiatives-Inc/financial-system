## 3. CAPITAL STACK & FUNDING SOURCES

### AHP (Advocates for Human Potential) Credit Facility Proposal (June 2, 2025)

**AHP Equity Tranche:**
- AHP Credit Facility: $3,500,000 at 4% APR (from long-term savings reserves)
- Status: Obligated but not yet issued
- Structure: Tranche from long-term savings, currently earning 4% in Money Market

**Key Funding Structure:**
1. AHP provides $3,500,000 credit facility at 4% APR (backup equity from long-term reserves)
2. DFC (Duty First Capital) establishes Private Equity (PE) fund with initial backup equity of $3,500,000
3. With full project funding already in hand via grants/grantees, project is fully funded
4. Nonprofit and team apply for grants and tax credits
5. Every dollar raised is a dollar NOT drawn down from $3.5M PE fund
6. AHP's investment structured like HELOC - funds drawn as needed, only then generating cost

**Funding Timeline Advantage:**
- Full project funding already in hand = no project risk for grantees, investors, local donors
- Much more willing to fund and support project
- NONPROFIT and team apply for grants and tax credits, paying down actual cost of rehab
- Approach yields enormous timing and cost advantages

## 7. STRUCTURAL REQUIREMENTS & TECHNICAL CONSTRAINTS

### Roof Framing Requirements:
- Snow Load: Existing inadequate for current code (35 psf); sister with 2x10 for side gables, 2x12 for main gable
- Existing: 2x7 rough-cut rafters at 19" on center (main gable), 2x6 at 24" on center (side gables)
- Ceiling/collar ties must remain at current elevations
- Hurricane ties required: H8 at main gable, H2.5A at side gables

### Attic Floor Framing (Third Floor):
- Existing: 2x7 or 2x6 joists at 16" on center
- Required for occupied space: 40 psf at units, 80 psf at corridors
- Solution: Sister each joist with new 2x8 joist
- Subfloor: Replace wood planks with 3/4" thick plywood
- Floor-to-wall ties: Install continuous 2x cleats and H8 ties

### Basement Constraints:
- **Ceiling Height Requirement:** Minimum 6 feet 8 inches (2032 mm) for habitable/accessory spaces
- **Finding:** Only two specific basement areas currently meet this requirement
- **Compliant Uses:** Mechanical systems, storage, laundry (current plan ~723 SF)
- **Non-Compliant Areas:** Proposed gym/wellness room (940 SF) would require excavation/slab lowering
- **Cost Implication:** Basement expansion "would substantially elevate project costs, potentially by tens of thousands of dollars per affected zone"

### Energy Code Compliance:
- **Stretch Energy Code (225 CMR 22.00):** Requires HERS index of 42 (or 45 with embodied carbon credits)
- **Historic Building Exemption:** 75 Oliver Street eligible for exemptions/modifications to full energy code compliance if strict adherence would threaten historic fabric
- **Proposed Measures:** Enhanced insulation (attics/basements without compromising historic elements), high-efficiency systems (mini-splits), central heat-pump hot water, EV-ready infrastructure, solar-ready zones

### Means of Egress Requirements:
- Dual egress required for R-2 occupancy
- Existing stairs viable with strategic fire-rated upgrades to 1-hour rating
- Third floor (attic): Requires exterior egress (fire escape stair or balcony system)
- **MHC Coordination Required:** Any exterior modifications must be coordinated with Massachusetts Historical Commission for Certificate of Appropriateness; approval timeline 30-60 days

## 8. FINANCIAL CONSTRAINTS & RISK FACTORS

### Identified Constraints:

1. **Capital Needs Gap (2020 Assessment):** $1,500,000+ in necessary repairs identified

2. **Abatement Not Included:** Proposal allowance of $50,000 does NOT include actual abatement costs if contamination (lead, asbestos, mold, soil) discovered

3. **Basement Headroom Limitation:** Non-compliant areas would require tens of thousands per zone for excavation to make habitable

4. **Historic Preservation Constraints:**
   - Exterior modifications require MHC approval (30-60 days)
   - Fire stairs/egress may face preservation conflicts
   - Facade, windows, and porch modifications limited by historic register provisions
   - All exterior work must preserve historic integrity

5. **Zoning & Permitting:** May require variances, special permits, or 40B process (requires zoning analysis and attorney)

6. **Parking:** Available space limited; resident and staff parking needs to be minimized

7. **Property Condition Uncertainties:**
   - Latent issues may exist beyond visible structural assessment
   - Geotechnical evaluation may be warranted for foundation modifications
   - Floor levelness issues noted at main gable (may require additional reinforcing)

8. **Inflation & Market Conditions:** Estimates assume standard materials and labor rates as of December 2025; inflation and supply chain issues could impact final costs
